Introduction

This study guide has been tailored specifically for your medical university chemistry oral exam based on the materials provided by your professor. The exam will cover three main areas:

  1. Inorganic Chemistry
  2. Organic Chemistry
  3. Biochemistry Propedeutics

Your professor will ask one question from each area, with special emphasis on electrochemistry, kinetics, thermodynamics, carbohydrates, and NAD/FAD structures. The exam typically lasts 40-60 minutes.

Remember that your professor will ask you to draw various reactions and structures, so practice drawing is essential. Pay special attention to graphs and visual elements, as emphasized in your professor's guide.

How to Use This Study Guide

  1. Follow the 20-day study schedule, allocating approximately 4-6 hours per day
  2. For each topic:
    • Read the content thoroughly
    • Study the associated mnemonics and memory aids
    • Review the practical examples
    • Practice drawing key structures and reactions
    • Test yourself using the interactive quizzes
  3. Regularly review previously covered material
  4. In the final days, focus on comprehensive review and practice explaining concepts aloud
  5. Use the interactive tools to enhance your learning experience

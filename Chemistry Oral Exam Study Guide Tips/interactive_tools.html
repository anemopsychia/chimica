What is the First Law of Thermodynamics?

The First Law of Thermodynamics states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred or converted from one form to another. Mathematically: ΔU = q + w

What is Gibbs Free Energy and how does it relate to spontaneity?

Gibbs Free Energy (G) is defined as G = H - TS. A process is spontaneous when ΔG < 0, at equilibrium when ΔG = 0, and non-spontaneous when ΔG > 0.

What is Hess's Law?

Hess's Law states that the enthalpy change of a reaction is independent of the pathway between the initial and final states. It allows us to calculate enthalpy changes for reactions that cannot be measured directly.

What are the components of NAD+?

NAD+ consists of: 1) Nicotinamide (from vitamin B3), 2) Adenine, 3) Two ribose sugars, 4) Two phosphate groups connected by a pyrophosphate bridge.

What is the difference between NAD+ and NADP+?

NADP+ has an additional phosphate group attached to the 2'-hydroxyl of the adenosine ribose. Functionally, NAD+/NADH is primarily involved in catabolic reactions, while NADP+/NADPH is primarily involved in anabolic reactions.

What are the components of FAD?

FAD consists of: 1) Flavin (isoalloxazine ring from vitamin B2), 2) Ribitol (sugar alcohol), 3) Adenine, 4) Ribose sugar, 5) Two phosphate groups.
